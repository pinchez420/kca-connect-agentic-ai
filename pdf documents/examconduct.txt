# KCA University (KCAU) Academic Conduct Regulations

## 1. Academic Calendar and Graduation
*   **Academic Year:** Begins in September and ends in August of the subsequent year.
*   **Structure:** Consists of three trimesters or two semesters. Each term is fifteen (15) weeks long.
*   **Graduation Ceremony:** Time and place are decided by the University Senate.

## 2. Admissions Policy
*   **General Criteria:** Admission is based on meeting minimum entry requirements set by the University Senate and space availability.
*   **Equality of Opportunity:** KCAU does not discriminate based on ethnicity, nationality, disability, sexual orientation, gender, religion, or socio-economic background.
*   **Regulatory Oversight:** Criteria are proposed by the Deans Committee and approved by the Senate and the Commission for University Education (CUE).

## 3. Student Registration
### 3.1 New Students
*   **Requirements:** Must present Admission letters.
*   **Process:** Students fill out a KCAU registration form for subjects/units. They are issued a Student ID (Smart Card) after fee payment.
*   **Official Names:** Names must match application documents or National ID. Name changes during study require legal procedures and must be completed before graduation.

### 3.2 Continuing Students
*   **Process:** Must register for units every trimester/semester via the Student Portal (www.kca.ac.ke).
*   **Pre-registration:** Begins 4 weeks before the new term starts.

### 3.3 Add/Drop and Deadlines
*   **Add/Drop (Swap) Units:** Must be done within the first 2 weeks of the term.
*   **Academic Leave:** Students unable to register must apply for academic leave by Week 6 of the trimester.

## 4. Deferred Admission and Academic Leave
*   **Reservation:** Admission slots can be reserved for a maximum of three trimesters (one academic year).
*   **Deferment Process:** Requires a "Student Request Form" from the Registrarâ€™s office or portal.
*   **Continuing Students:** May be granted academic leave for a maximum of two academic years (applied for one year at a time). 
*   **Deregistration:** Students who fail to register or apply for leave within deadlines will be deregistered.

## 5. Credit Transfers and Exemptions
*   **Eligibility:** Students from CUE-recognized institutions may apply.
*   **Maximum Credits:** Transferable credits cannot exceed 49% of the programme requirements.
*   **Fees:** A fee is charged per credit transferred (KCAU graduates are exempt from these specific fees).
*   **Change of Programme:** Students must complete requirements of their current programme before moving to a higher one.

## 6. Modes of Study
*   **Module I (Full-time):** Scheduled day classes with standard load.
*   **Module II (Part-time):** Scheduled evening classes.
*   **Module III (Weekend):** Scheduled weekend classes.
*   **Module IV (Distance Learning - DL):** Online materials, assignments, and tutorials; exams are held in person at approved venues.

## 7. Course Progression and Attendance
*   **Progression:** Students must satisfy progression rules. Those with supplementary results or disciplinary issues cannot proceed until cleared.
*   **Class Attendance:** Compulsory. Students must not miss more than four weeks of a term. Attendance registers must be signed for all units.

## 8. Examination Conduct and Regulations
### 8.1 General Rules
*   **Arrival:** Students must be in the exam room 15 minutes before the start.
*   **Entry/Exit:** No entry after 30 minutes; no exit until 30 minutes before the end.
*   **Calculators:** Must be electronic, portable, noiseless, and non-programmable. No operation manuals or "smart" gadgets allowed.

### 8.2 Exam Types
*   **Open Book:** Students may bring specified printed materials/books. No materials may be attached to the script.
*   **Restricted Book:** Only materials specified by the examiner are allowed.

### 8.3 Special and Supplementary Examinations
*   **Supplementary Exams:** For those who fail a unit. If failed again, the unit must be repeated.
*   **Special Exams (Admissible Reasons):** 
    1. Bereavement of nuclear family.
    2. Sickness (Doctor's note required).
    3. Work-related international travel (2 weeks notice).
    4. Registered fee defaulters.
*   **Special Exams (Inadmissible Reasons):** Misreading the timetable or lateness due to transport (these students must repeat the unit).

## 9. Appeals and Results
*   **Appeals:** Must be made in writing within 2 weeks of result release. A non-refundable fee is required.
*   **Remarking:** The final grade after a remark is binding (even if lower than the original).
*   **Transcripts:** Available via the student portal. Certificates are issued once; duplicates are not provided, only letters of confirmation.

## 10. Graduation and Clearance
*   **Eligibility:** All academic and financial obligations must be cleared.
*   **Storage Fees:** Certificates and transcripts should be collected within one year; thereafter, storage charges apply.
