# AMENDED CONSTITUTION OF THE STUDENTS ASSOCIATION OF KCA UNIVERSITY (SAKU) - 2021

## PREAMBLE & AUTHORITY
- **SAKU Authority:** This constitution is the legal instrument binding KCA University students across all campuses. 
- **Supreme Authority:** Recognized under the Universities Act No. 42 of 2012 and KCA University Statutes.
- **Core Purpose:** To safeguard students' rights regardless of creed, nationality, race, gender, or economic status.

## CHAPTER 1: NAME, DEFINITIONS, AND OBJECTS
- **Official Name:** Students Association of KCA University (SAKU).
- **Key Definition - SAKU Council:** The central leadership organ constituted under Article 3.2.
- **Key Definition - Ordinary Member:** A registered student who has paid the required trimester fees.
- **Key Definition - Trimester:** A period of study as set by KCAU management.
- **Objectives of SAKU:** 
    1. Promote student welfare and safeguard rights.
    2. Ensure student participation in University organs.
    3. Foster student-lecturer relationships and leadership qualities.
    4. Raise funds through legal, University-approved means.

## CHAPTER 2: MEMBERSHIP AND RIGHTS
- **Membership Classes:** SAKU has three classes: 1. Ordinary, 2. Associate, 3. General.
- **Ordinary Membership Eligibility:** Open to all registered students who are paid up for the trimester.
- **Rights of Ordinary Members:** Entitled to vote in SAKU elections, seek office, and access Association records (books of accounts, minutes).
- **Cessation of Membership:** Ends upon graduation, failure to register for a trimester, death, expulsion, or transfer to another university.

## CHAPTER 3: LEADERSHIP AND THE SAKU COUNCIL
- **Leadership Structure:** Administered at two levels: 1. SAKU Council (Main Ruaraka Campus), 2. Campus Leadership Committees (CLC) for Regional Campuses.
- **SAKU Council Composition:** 
    1. Chairperson
    2. Vice-Chairperson (Must be opposite gender to Chair)
    3. Secretary General
    4. Treasurer (Position reserved for women)
    5. Academic Secretary
    6. Sports and Entertainment Secretary
    7. Special Interest Secretary
- **Campus Leadership Committee (CLC):** Includes Regional Chairperson, Regional Finance Secretary (reserved for women), and Regional Secretary.

### Roles and Duties
- **SAKU Chairperson Duty:** Overall leader, chairs Council and General Meetings, represents student interests to University Administration.
- **SAKU Vice-Chairperson Duty:** Deputizes the Chair, handles academic grievances, and ensures SAKU Council harmony.
- **SAKU Secretary General Duty:** Official spokesperson, maintains the register of members, and keeps records of all proceedings.
- **SAKU Treasurer Duty:** Custodian of books of account, mandatory signatory for bank accounts, and implements the two-thirds gender rule in financial affairs.

## CHAPTER 4: MEETINGS AND QUORUM
- **Meeting Types:** Annual General Meeting (AGM), Special General Meeting (SGM), and Extra-ordinary General Meeting (Kamukunji).
- **Quorum for General Meetings:** Two hundred (200) members or one-quarter of Ordinary Members, whichever is attainable.
- **AGM Timeline:** Held not later than 12 months after the previous AGM.

## CHAPTER 5: ELECTIONS AND THE ELECTORAL COLLEGE
- **Election Date:** Held on the second last Tuesday of October annually.
- **The Electoral College:** Students elect representatives from academic departments/faculties. These representatives then elect the SAKU Council.
- **Qualification to Contest:**
    1. Must be a registered Ordinary Member with no fee balance.
    2. Must have a Cumulative GPA of 2.0 (C average) or above for two preceding semesters.
    3. Must submit a Certificate of Good Conduct from DCI.
    4. Must not have been found guilty of a disciplinary offence.
- **IECK (Independent Electoral Commission of KCAU):** Appointed by the DVC Academic and Student Affairs to oversee free and fair elections.

## CHAPTER 6: FINANCE AND REVENUE
- **Revenue Sources:** Subscription fees, University allocations, donations, and approved business premises.
- **Financial Year:** Runs from November to October.
- **Audit:** SAKU accounts are subject to audit by the University Auditor; reports are tabled at the AGM.
- **Remuneration:** Executive officials are entitled to monthly allowances not exceeding 30% of total SAKU income.

## CHAPTER 7 & 8: DISPUTES AND IMPEACHMENT
- **Dispute Resolution:** First through Mediation (Dean of Students), then Arbitration (University Legal Counsel), then DVC Academic Affairs.
- **Vote of No Confidence:** Requires a petition supported by 1/4 of the Campus members or 2/3 of the SAKU Council. Requires a 2/3 majority vote of members present.

## CHAPTER 11: BILL OF RIGHTS
- **Student Rights:** 
    1. Freedom of thought and expression.
    2. Freedom from discrimination (creed, color, ethnicity, disability, gender).
    3. Right to privacy (personal property and communication).
    4. Right to fair hearing and representation in disciplinary cases.
    5. Right to a quiet environment and academic freedom.
